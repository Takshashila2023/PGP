To the Board & Executive Leadership

The Rules of the Game
Have Changed

Global trade is fragmenting. Tariff regimes are shifting faster than supply chains can adjust. Data localisation laws, sectoral licensing requirements, and ESG-linked compliance mandates are affecting boardroom-level decisions. Geopolitical flashpoints — from the Indo-Pacific to Eastern Europe — cascade directly into procurement costs, market access, and strategic partnerships.


In this environment, every C-suite executive is de facto a student of public policy — whether they chose to be or not. The question is no longer whether government decisions will affect your business. The question is whether your leadership team has the strategic confidence, the institutional knowledge, and the analytical vocabulary to act ahead of policy shifts, rather than react to them.


The Takshashila Institution's Post Graduate Programme in Public Policy (PGP) was designed precisely for this moment. We invite you to sponsor your senior executives and high-potential leaders through India's most rigorous practitioner-focused policy education.

The Business Case

Why Policy Literacy
Is a Competitive Advantage

Executives who understand how governments think build companies that are resilient, credible, and strategically agile. Those who don't, wait for the newspaper.

🌐
Geopolitical Headwinds

Executives who want to be ahead of the curve need to learn how to read the geopolitical map, not just the trade data. Supply chain rewiring, China+1 strategies, US tariff escalation, and friend-shoring need to be better understood in boardrooms.

⚖️
Regulatory Complexity

The compliance burden has moved from legal teams to the boardroom — from DPDP to PLI schemes, from SEBI disclosures to competition regulation. C-suite executives need to understand it all.

🏛️
Government as Stakeholder

Engagement is no longer optional for executives — it is strategic. Central ministries, state governments, and regulators are now active co-participants in industry strategy and corporates need to leverage these stakeholders better.

📊
Policy-Driven Market Shifts

Demand curves and competitive dynamics are being re-shaped in ways that MBAs alone cannot equip you for. Fiscal policy, monetary tightening, tax reform, and industrial policy — each needs to be understood better to be able to respond to them better.

"A business leader who does not understand how policy is made is like a general who does not understand terrain. The battlefield is the state, and its contours change constantly."

Nitin Pai · Co-Founder & Director, Takshashila Institution

Programme Structure

Curriculum Modules of
Direct Corporate Relevance

Spanning three terms across 48 weekends, the PGP is delivered via live & interactive online sessions and three in-person workshops across India. Below are the modules most directly applicable to corporate and boardroom decision-making.

Geopolitics
International Relations & Foreign Affairs
  • Understanding geopolitics and the international system
  • Tools of statecraft: foreign policy & diplomacy
  • Technology's role in reshaping global power
  • Supply chain implications of great-power competition
Economics
How Nations Prosper
  • Indian economy in global context
  • Institutions, jobs & employment policy
  • Government revenue, expenditure & subsidies
  • Key economic debates of the last decade
Regulation
Microeconomics & Market Regulation
  • Market failures & government intervention
  • Subsidies, price controls, quotas & tariffs
  • Public goods and externality
  • Behavioural economics for policy design
Finance
Macroeconomics & Monetary Policy
  • Growth cycles & economic fluctuations
  • Monetary policy and exchange rate regimes
  • Capital controls & the impossible trinity
  • Fiscal policy and its business implications
Governance
Constitutional Structure & Policy Making
  • How Parliament and ministries make laws
  • Union–State–Local government dynamics
  • Legal basis of regulatory power
  • How executives engage with legislative process
Governance
Masterclass in Effective Governance
  • Understand the core principles of effective governance including accountability, transparency, institutional design, and incentives
  • Analyse policy implementation challenges and diagnose why well-designed policies often fail during implementation
  • Apply evidence-based governance tools using data, impact evaluation insights, and behavioural approaches to design governance reforms
  • Improve outcomes in areas such as education, health, and social programs
Communication
Writing a Policy Brief
  • Structuring evidence for policy audiences
  • Constructing credible alternatives
  • Trade-off analysis for decision makers
  • Communicating to Ministers, Secretaries & Boards
Public Finance
Introduction to Public Finance
  • Tax policy and reforms in India
  • Fiscal decentralisation & federalism
  • Macroeconomics of public finance
  • Impact of government spending on industry
